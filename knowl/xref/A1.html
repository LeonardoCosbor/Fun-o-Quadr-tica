Item 1.
Seja a função quadrática \(f(x)=3(x-2)^2\text{.}\) Analise os valores de y para \(x\in\{-4,-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3,4\}\text{,}\) mostrando quais são as coordenadas do vértice e quais são os valores de \(x\) possuem os mesmos valores de \(y\text{.}\)
Solução.
Logo,
\begin{equation*} f(-4)=3\cdot(-4-2)^2=3\cdot36=108 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-3)=3\cdot(-3-2)^2=3\cdot25=75 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-2)=3\cdot(-2-2)^2=3\cdot16=48 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-1)=3\cdot(-1-2)^2=3\cdot9=27 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(0)=3\cdot(0-2)^2=3\cdot4=12 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(1)=3\cdot(1-2)^2=3\cdot1=3 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(2)=3\cdot(2-2)^2=3\cdot0=0 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(3)=3\cdot(3-2)^2=3\cdot1=3 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(4)=3\cdot(4-2)^2=3\cdot4=12 \end{equation*}
Note que os valores de y começam a repetir a partir do ponto \((2,0)\text{.}\) Isto significa que este ponto é o vértice da parábola. Logo,
\begin{equation*} f(2)=0\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(1)=f(3)=3\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(0)=f(4)=12\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-1)=f(5)=27\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-2)=f(6)=48\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f(-3)=f(7)=75 \end{equation*}
e
\begin{equation*} f(-4)=f(8)=108\text{.} \end{equation*}
